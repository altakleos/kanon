# Spec: Contract resolutions

## Intent

Define the substrate's runtime-binding artifact: `.kanon/resolutions.yaml`. A resolution is the cached output of an LLM agent reading a prose contract and inspecting the consumer repo, recording what concrete invocation realizes the contract in this repo, with evidence-grounded provenance and version-pinned freshness. The kernel replays cached resolutions mechanically; the resolver runs only on developer machines.

Per [ADR-0039](../decisions/0039-contract-resolution-model.md), resolutions are how the substrate composes prose contracts (kit-shipped, project-defined, or third-party) over consumer-specific repos without baking ecosystem knowledge into the kernel and without violating [`P-runtime-non-interception`](../foundations/principles/P-runtime-non-interception.md).

## Invariants

<!-- INV-resolutions-machine-only-owned -->
1. **Machine-only-owned.** `.kanon/resolutions.yaml` is generated by the resolver and replayed by the kernel; it is not hand-edited. Every entry carries a `meta-checksum:` field covering the entry's own bytes; the kernel computes and compares at replay time. A mismatch surfaces as a `code: hand-edit-detected` finding, and the kernel refuses replay until re-resolution lands.

<!-- INV-resolutions-quadruple-pin -->
2. **Quadruple version-pinning.** Every resolution entry pins all four of: `contract-version` (the contract's `semantic-version:` frontmatter), `contract-content-SHA` (SHA-256 of the contract file's full bytes), `resolver-model` (the model identifier used at resolution time), and `evidence-SHA` (per-cited-evidence-file SHA-256 at resolution time). Drift on any pin invalidates the entry and triggers re-resolution.

<!-- INV-resolutions-evidence-grounded -->
3. **Evidence-grounded.** Every entry cites at least one repo evidence file the agent read to determine the realization. Cited paths must exist on disk at replay time; cited content-SHAs must match current SHAs (modulo INV-resolutions-stale-fails). An entry with zero cited evidence is rejected at replay with `code: ungrounded-resolution`.

<!-- INV-resolutions-replay-deterministic -->
4. **Replay-deterministic.** Two replays of the same `.kanon/resolutions.yaml` against the same repo state (same evidence-file bytes, same kernel version) produce byte-equal JSON output, excluding monotonic timestamps. This is the substrate-determinism falsification probe (P2 per `de-opinionation.md`); failure is P0-severity.

<!-- INV-resolutions-resolver-not-in-ci -->
5. **Resolver runs only on developer machines.** CI replays cached resolutions; CI does not invoke the resolver. The kernel ships no LLM client; `kanon resolve` (the resolver-invocation CLI) is a thin layer that hands off to the consumer's installed agent harness. CI configurations that invoke `kanon resolve` are a configuration violation, surfaced (where detectable) by `kanon verify` as `code: resolver-in-ci`.

<!-- INV-resolutions-stale-fails -->
6. **Stale fails, never silent.** SHA mismatch on any pin or missing cited evidence file surfaces as a `kanon verify` finding (`code: sha-mismatch`, `code: missing-evidence`, `code: stale-resolution`) and the kernel refuses to claim conformance. Stale state never silently passes.

## Rationale

These six invariants together codify the substrate's commitment that **the contract is the source; the resolution is the derived cache**. Without invariant 1 (machine-only ownership), the resolution becomes hand-authored configuration and prose-as-code is undermined. Without invariant 2 (quadruple-pinning), drift between contract evolution and consumer-side caching goes undetected. Without invariant 3 (evidence-grounding), the resolver's output cannot be audited and the prose-as-code chain breaks at the binding layer. Without invariant 4 (replay-determinism), the kernel cannot certify anything about what the consumer's CI just executed. Without invariant 5 (resolver-not-in-CI), the substrate's trust boundary widens to include LLM execution against unmerged content. Without invariant 6 (stale fails), the cache silently outlives its grounding.

The invariants are deliberately structural and falsifiable — Phase A's test suite proves each by construction. None claim semantic correctness of the agent's resolution choices (per the verification-contract spec's INV-11 disclosure); structural coherence is the kernel's job.

## Out of Scope

- **Composition algebra** — `surface:`, `before/after:` ordering across contracts targeting the same execution surface, `replaces:` substitution. Ratified by ADR-0041 (realization-shape + grammar).
- **Realization-shape schema** — per-contract frontmatter declaring allowed verbs, evidence kinds, stage keys. Ratified by ADR-0041.
- **Dialect grammar versioning** — `kanon-dialect:` pin; date-stamped immutable artifacts. Ratified by ADR-0041.
- **Recipe definition** — recipes are publisher-shipped target-tree YAML opting consumers into multiple contracts at once. Ratified by ADR-0043 (distribution + cadence).
- **Migration script** — `kanon migrate v0.3 → v0.4`. Phase A territory.
- **Semantic correctness of resolutions** — whether the agent's choice of invocation actually realizes the contract's intent. Outside the kernel's mechanical verification boundary; surfaced by INV-11 in `verification-contract.md`; addressed by human review and (in subsequent ADRs) cross-publisher fidelity fixtures.
